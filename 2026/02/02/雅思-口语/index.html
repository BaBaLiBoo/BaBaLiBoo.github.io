Part 1 (8-12 questions, 4-5min)

Food

What is your favourite food?

I love lots of food, but if I had to pick one, it would definitely be hotpot. The best thing about it is that you can try a wide variety of ingredients in one meal. Unlike normal dishes, you can just put everything you like into the pot. It’s also perfect for spicy food lovers like me, because it really helps me relax after a long day.

What kind of food did you like when you were young?

When I was a kid, I was crazy about fast food, especially KFC. Fried chicken, burgers and fries just smelled amazing to me. But my parents didn’t take me there very often, so it always felt like a special treat. I even joked that I wanted to open my own KFC when I grew up.

Do you eat different foods at different times of the year?

Yes, I usually eat different kinds of food depending on the season. In summer, I prefer cold noodles or mung bean soup because they help me cool down. But in winter, I tend to eat warmer food like hotpot or soup since they make me feel cozy.

Has your favourite food changed since you were a child?

Yes, it has changed quite a lot. When I was young, I preferred fast food, but now I find it a bit too greasy. These days, I prefer hotpot because it feels healthier and more satisfying, especially when the weather is cold.

Pets and Animals

What’s your favourite animal? Why?

My favorite animal is the capybara. It’s a large rodent from South America, and it recently became really popular online. I saw many cute videos of it, and it always looks calm and friendly. I think its relaxed personality is really comforting, which is probably why I like it so much.

Where do you prefer to keep your pet, indoors or outdoors?

I don’t actually have a pet, but if I did, I’d probably prefer keeping it outdoors. I feel animals are happier when they have more space and fresh air. Staying indoors all day might be a bit boring for them.

Have you ever had a pet before?

Only fish, actually. My mum and I are a bit afraid of animals, so we never kept cats or dogs. We had some goldfish when I was little, and I was responsible for feeding them. But they died quite soon, and I felt really sad. Since then, I haven’t kept any pets. Separation is too heartbreaking for me.

What is the most popular animal in China?

Recently, I’d say capybaras are surprisingly popular. Because of social media and some viral videos, many people find them cute and relaxing. You can even see capybara toys and emojis everywhere now.

Sports team

Have you ever been part of a sports team?

Yes, it was a long time ago when I was in primary school. I used to be a part of the school track and field team. I really enjoyed training and taking part in competitions, and that was one of my happiest memories of my childhood.

But after I graduated from primary school, I never joined any sports team again. The main reason was the heavy study load in middle school. I spent all my time on study.

But to be honest, I feel a bit regretful about this. I think joining the sports team is really meaningful. It can not only keep your fit, but also help you develop teamwork and a positive attitude.

Is team sports popular in your culture?

I’d say team sports are popular for watching but not so popular for actually playing. Many people enjoy watching basketball or volleyball games, especially big competitions. But in daily life, only a small number of people really participate, mainly because students are under heavy study load and don’t have much free time.

Do you like watching team games? Why?

Yeah, definitely. I’m a big fan of volleyball, so I often stay up late to watch matches with my family. I think team sports are more exciting to watch because it’s not only about individual skills, but also teamwork. That combination makes the game much more attractive.

What are the differences between team sports and individual sports?

I think individual sports focus more on personal skills and ability, because everything depends on one person. But team sports are different. They rely more on teamwork and cooperation among teammates. Personally, I find team sports more exciting to watch.

2：1 topic, 准备 1min，说 1-2min

3：深入2，双向讨论，4-5min

完美工作

P2

Describe a perfect job you would like to have in the future

You should say:

What it is

How you knew it

What you need to learn to get this job

And explain why you think it is a perfect job for you

To me, my ideal job would be a robotics algorithm researcher. Basically, this role is like the brain behind a robot, because you design the algorithms that control how it thinks and moves.

I first learned about this job at university when I was studying in my tutor’s robotics lab. Watching senior students design and test robots really inspired me, and that’s when I realized this was what I wanted to do in the future.

To get this job, I still have a lot to learn. I need strong coding skills and a solid foundation in maths, machine learning and control systems. Besides that, teamwork is also very important, because developing a robot usually requires close cooperation with engineers and researchers from different fields.

I think it’s a perfect job for me for several reasons. First, I’m not a very outgoing person, so I prefer focusing deeply on research rather than constantly dealing with people. Second, robotics is a meaningful and promising field that can really benefit society, so I hope to make it my future career. Besides, it also offers stable and decent pay, which can support a comfortable life. So overall, it’s both interesting and practical for me.

P3

What kind of job can be called a ‘dream job’?

First of all, a dream job should be something you’re genuinely interested in, so that you can gain a sense of satisfaction and personal growth from it. Besides that, it should allow you to maintain a healthy work-life balance, which means finishing work on time and spending evenings with your family. Finally, it should provide a stable income. It doesn’t have to be extremely high, but it should be enough to support a comfortable life.

What jobs do children want to do when they grow up?

I think most children are attracted to jobs that look cool or meaningful rather than practical. For example, many kids want to be doctors, teachers, police officers or scientists, because these jobs seem heroic and respected. At that age, they don’t really think about salary or job pressure. They just follow their imagination.

Do people’s ideal jobs change as they grow up?

Yes, I think most people’s ideal jobs change over time. When we’re children, we usually choose jobs based on interest or fantasy. But as we grow older, we start to think more realistically about income, stability and work-life balance. So our decisions become more practical. Of course, there are still a few people who stick to their childhood dreams, but they’re probably the minority.

What should people consider when choosing jobs?

I think there are three main factors.

iterest

work-life balance

salary

So ideally, people should find a balance among these factors rather than focusing on only one.

Is salary the main reason why people choose a certain job?

I’d say it depends on the person. For many people, especially those who have financial pressure, salary is definitely a key factor, because money provides security and stability. However, for others, job satisfaction or personal interest may be more important. So I don’t think salary is the only reason, but it’s certainly a very practical one.

What kind of jobs are the most popular in your country?

AI / algorithm——>high salary

a job for life(teacher, doctor,civil servant)——> stable

想见的名人

P2

Describe a famous person you would like to meet

You should say:

Who he/she is

How you knew him/her

How/where you would like to meet him/her

And explain why you would like to meet him/her

The famous person I’d really like to meet is Zhu Ting. She used to be the captain of the Chinese women’s volleyball team and is considered one of the best players in the world.

I first got to know her during the 2016 Rio Olympics. I remember staying up late to watch the matches with my family, and Zhu Ting almost carried the whole team on her shoulders. Her performance on the court was just incredible, and finally helped China win the gold medal. That was when I truly became a huge fan of her.

If I had the chance, I’d love to meet her in my university’s volleyball gym. I actually took a volleyball course in college because of her, so it would be amazing to play a game with her or maybe just talk to her face to face.

The main reason I want to meet her is that she has influenced me a lot. I’ve read many interviews about her life, and she went through so many injuries and setbacks but never gave up. Her resilience and mental strength really inspire me whenever I face difficulties in my own life. So for me, meeting her wouldn’t just be exciting, it would also be very meaningful.
